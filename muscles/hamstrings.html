Hamstrings

Muscle Function

The hamstrings comprise three muscles: the semitendinosus, semimembranosus, and biceps femoris, primarily responsible for knee flexion. The semimembranosus and semitendinosus facilitate hip extension, knee flexion, and internal rotation of the lower leg when the knee is flexed. The biceps femoris contributes to hip extension, knee flexion, and external rotation of the lower leg during slight knee flexion, and aids in external rotation of the thigh when the hip is extended. Collectively, the hamstrings play a crucial role in knee movement and locomotion.

Exercises

Since the hamstrings are responsible for knee flexion and hip extension, you should perform exercises that involve these movements.

  • Knee flexion:
    • Seated leg curls
    • Lying leg curls
    • Nordic curls
    • Standing leg curls
  • Hip extension:
    • Romanian deadlifts
    • Straight leg deadlifts
    • Deadlifts

Pick one exercise for knee flexion and one for hip extension and do 2-3 sets for each exercise based on your preference.
