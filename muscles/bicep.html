Biceps Brachii

Muscle Function

The biceps brachii is a large muscle located in the anterior compartment of the upper arm. It is a two-headed muscle, consisting of a long head and a short head, both originating from the scapula (shoulder blade) and inserting onto the radius bone in the forearm. The biceps brachii is a primary flexor of the elbow joint, meaning it bends the arm at the elbow. Additionally, it assists in forearm supination (rotating the palm upward) and plays a role in shoulder flexion (lifting the arm forward). Due to its dual-headed structure, the biceps brachii can generate significant force, making it a key muscle for various upper body movements.

Exercises

Here are some exercises that target the biceps:

  • Barbell curls
  • Dumbbell curls
  • Hammer curls
  • Preacher curls
  • Concentration curls
