Pectoralis Major

Muscle Function

The pectoralis major, also known as the chest, is the largest muscle of the anterior chest wall. The chest contains two main heads, the clavicular head (upper chest) and the sternal head (mid-low chest). The main function of the chest is flexion, adduction, and medial rotation of the arm at the glenohumeral joint (main joint of the shoulder). The clavicular head primarily functions to flex the humerus, which is lifting the arm forward and upward. While the sternal head’s main function is to adduct, medially rotate, and extend the humerus. The sternal head pulls the arms towards the body and rotates the humerus inward.

Exercises

It is in your best interest to do two chest exercises, one that biases the clavicular head and one that biases the sternal head.

  • Clavicular Head Exercises:
    • Incline smith machine press
    • Incline dumbbell/barbell press
    • Low-to-high cable fly
  • Sternal Head Exercises:
    • Pec-deck Machine
    • Flat smith machine press
    • Flat Dumbbell/Bench Press

Pick one exercise from each list and do 2-3 sets for each exercise based on your preference.
