Erector Spinae

Muscle Function

The erector spinae is a large, superficial muscle located just beneath the thoracolumbar fascia, originating from the erector spinae aponeurosis. It consists of the iliocostalis, longissimus, and spinalis muscles. Its primary function is to extend the vertebral column through bilateral contraction, straightening the back. Additionally, it enables lateral flexion, supports upright posture, and controls forward bending. Together, these muscles straighten the spine from a flexed position and facilitate backward bending.

Exercises

The erector spinae muscles are primarily trained through exercises that involve extending the vertebral column. Thus, any hip hinge movement will train the erector spinae.

  • Back extensions
  • Romanian deadlifts
  • Straight leg deadlifts
  • Deadlifts
  • Good mornings
  • Reverse hyperextensions

Pick one exercise and do 2-3 sets based on your preference.
