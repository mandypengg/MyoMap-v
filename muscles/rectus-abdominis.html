Rectus Abdominis

Muscle Function

The rectus abdominis is the top layer of your abdominal muscles, is commonly referred to as the "six-pack," and is the most visible portion of the abs. The rectus abdominis consists of two flat parallel muscles separated by connective tissue that extends along the whole length of both sides of the abdomen. The function of the rectus abdominis is to flex the spinal column and tense the anterior abdominal wall and assist in compressing the abdominal contents. It also plays a big role in core stability. Thus the strength of the abdominal muscles is important in health.

Exercises

Don't fall for those 30 minute circuits for a "six-pack". The rectus abdominis are a muscle just like any other, so why train them differently? Any good spinal flexion exercises will train the rectus abdominis.

  • Spinal Flexion Exercises:
    • Cable Crunches
    • Crunch Machine
    • Weighted Decline Crunches

Pick one exercise from the list and do 2-3 sets based on your preference.
